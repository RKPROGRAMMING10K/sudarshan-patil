Liver Specialist

A liver specialist is a physician who specializes in the diagnosis, treatment, and management of diseases and disorders related to the liver. These specialists are also known as hepatologists or liver doctors. Hepatologists undergo specialized training in hepatology after completing their medical degree and internal medicine residency.

Liver specialists have expertise in the following areas:

  • Liver diseases: They diagnose and treat a wide range of liver conditions, including viral hepatitis (such as hepatitis B and C), alcoholic liver disease, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), liver cirrhosis, liver cancer (hepatocellular carcinoma), autoimmune liver diseases, genetic liver diseases, and liver failure.
  • Liver function: They assess liver function and interpret liver function tests, which measure various enzymes, proteins, and substances in the blood to evaluate liver health.
  • Liver transplantation: Liver specialists are involved in the evaluation and management of patients who require liver transplantation. They play a crucial role in the pre-transplant assessment, selection of suitable candidates, post-transplant care, and monitoring of transplant recipients.
  • Hepatitis management: Hepatologists are experienced in the treatment of viral hepatitis, including the use of antiviral medications, monitoring of viral load, and assessment of liver damage and fibrosis progression.
  • Liver cancer: They participate in the diagnosis, staging, and management of liver cancer. This may involve coordinating with oncologists, surgeons, and other specialists to develop a comprehensive treatment plan, which may include surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, or ablation techniques.
  • Liver diseases: Various liver conditions, including hepatitis, cirrhosis, fatty liver disease, and liver cancer.
  • Interventional procedures: Some hepatologists are trained in performing interventional procedures for the liver, such as liver biopsies, paracentesis (fluid removal from the abdomen), and placement of transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunts (TIPS) for managing complications of liver disease.

    • Liver specialists work closely with a multidisciplinary team, including gastroenterologists, radiologists, oncologists, surgeons, and transplant coordinators, to provide comprehensive care for patients with liver conditions. They may also collaborate with nutritionists, social workers, and other healthcare professionals to address the broader aspects of patient care and support.
